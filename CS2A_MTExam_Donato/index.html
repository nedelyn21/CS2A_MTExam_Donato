Tourist Spots and Local Products of Ilocos Sur

Top Tourist Spots & Adventures

Sulvec Beach

- Sulvec Beach is known for its serene shoreline and clear waters, perfect for relaxation. The place is surrounded by natural rock formations that make it a unique seaside attraction. It is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and enjoying sunsets.

#SulvecBeach #IlocosSur #BeachVibes #NatureEscape #SunsetViews

Calle Crisologo

- Calle Crisologo is a famous heritage street in Vigan lined with Spanish-era houses. Its cobblestone roads and antique structures showcase the rich history of the Ilocos region. Tourists enjoy walking here, riding calesas, and buying souvenirs.

#CalleCrisologo #ViganHeritage #SpanishEra #CulturalTour #HistoricVigan

Aw-Asen Falls

- Aw-Asen Falls is a hidden gem in Ilocos Sur surrounded by lush greenery. The waterfall offers a refreshing escape for nature lovers and adventure seekers. It is a great place for swimming, picnics, and enjoying the cool mountain breeze.

#AwAsenFalls #NatureLovers #IlocosSurAdventures #WaterfallEscape #EcoTourism

Famous Local Products

Cacao chocolate

₱250 / 12pc

Cacao chocolate is made from locally grown cacao beans in Ilocos. It is rich, earthy, and often turned into tablea for hot drinks. This product supports local farmers and traditions.

Rambutan

₱

Rambutan is a tropical fruit with a hairy red shell and sweet flesh. It is usually eaten fresh but can also be used in desserts. This fruit is loved during harvest season for its refreshing taste.

Vigan Longganisa

₱1

Vigan Longganisa is a garlicky and sour native sausage. It is often paired with rice and sukang Iloko. This delicacy is a famous pasalubong from Vigan. is a garlicky and sour native sausage. It is often paired with rice and sukang Iloko. This delicacy is a famous pasalubong from Vigan.

Chicapop

₱50 / pack

Chicapop is a crispy snack made from popped chichacorn. It comes in different flavors like cheese, garlic, and barbecue. Tourists enjoy it as a fun and crunchy treat.

Royal Bibingka

₱200

Royal Bibingka is a sticky rice cake topped with cheese. It is denser and chewier than the regular bibingka. This sweet treat is common during holidays.

Sukang Iloko

₱300 / kilo

Sukang Iloko is vinegar made from fermented sugarcane. It is widely used in Ilocano cooking and dipping sauces. Its strong sour taste pairs well with fried dishes.

Bagnet

₱70

Bagnet is a deep-fried pork belly with crispy skin. It is crunchy outside but tender and juicy inside. This dish is a pride of Ilocos cuisine.

Ilocano bagoong

₱150

Ilocano bagoong is a fermented fish paste. It adds flavor to dishes like pinakbet and dinengdeng. It is a common condiment and pasalubong.

Daing

₱150

Daing is salted and sun-dried fish from coastal towns. It is usually fried and eaten with rice. This product shows the Ilocanos’ way of preserving food.

Ilocos Empanada

₱100 / kilo

Ilocos Empanada is a fried pastry with longganisa, egg, and vegetables. It has a crispy crust and is eaten with sukang Iloko. This is one of the most iconic street foods in Ilocos.

Bugnay Wine

₱120

Bugnay Wine is made from wild bugnay berries. It has a sweet, fruity taste and is often shared at gatherings. This drink reflects Ilocos’ traditional winemaking.

Banana

₱100 / pack

Bananas are widely grown in Ilocos. They are eaten fresh or cooked into snacks like turon and banana cue. This fruit is affordable and loved by locals.
